# Review a chapter of The OT Atlas

The atlas is compiled from published evidence with sources on every claim — but it is
**not yet practitioner-reviewed**, and it says so honestly. If you're an occupational
therapy practitioner, educator, or student, you can change that in about 30 minutes.

**Live site:** https://helperstudiostar.github.io/ot-atlas/

## How to review (≈30 min per chapter)

1. Pick a chapter you know well — Conditions, Assessments, or Treatments & protocols
   are the highest-value targets. Filter to your specialty area.
2. For each entry you read, check four things:
   - **Accuracy** — is the description clinically correct? Anything outdated or misleading?
   - **Evidence rating** — does the strong/moderate/emerging/contested/insufficient rating
     match your reading of the literature? (Each entry cites its key sources — spot-check one.)
   - **Framing** — is contested content presented fairly to both sides? Are cautions right?
   - **Omissions** — is anything important missing that a student would need?
3. File what you find as a [GitHub Issue](https://github.com/helperstudiostar/ot-atlas/issues/new?title=Review%3A%20)
   — one issue per chapter is fine; bullet points are fine; "this is all correct" is
   also a genuinely useful finding.
4. Include (optional, for the credits): your name as you'd like it shown, your credential
   (e.g., OTR/L, OTD, CHT), and which sections you reviewed.

## What reviewers get

Named credit in the atlas ("Reviewed by …" with credential and date) once your review is
incorporated. The goal is that every chapter eventually carries real practitioner eyes —
accumulated one review at a time, instead of gated behind one big sign-off.

## Ground rules for incorporating reviews

- Reviewer feedback that *cites evidence* wins over the compiled rating; feedback that
  *disagrees without evidence* gets logged in the entry's note as practitioner dissent.
- Evidence ratings only move when a source justifies the move (see HANDOFF.md — the same
  rule that binds the maintainers binds reviews).
- All changes land as commits referencing the review issue, so the trail is public.
